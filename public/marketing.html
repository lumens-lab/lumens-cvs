How it works

Money that moves
like a message.

Lumens eliminates the boundary between conversation and transaction. Here's how.

01

Create your account

Sign up with your phone number. Tiered KYC: Tier 1 requires only your SA ID or Ghana Card — takes under 2 minutes via Smile Identity. Tier 2 (higher limits) adds a biometric selfie. No paperwork.

02

Fund your wallet

Top up via bank EFT or card (Paystack SA), or MoMo (Ghana: MTN, Telecel, AirtelTigo via Zeepay). Deposit crypto directly to your non-custodial Stellar wallet. Your virtual card is live the moment your ZAR balance is positive.

03

Chat & send money

Open a chat with any Lumens contact. Tap 'Pay' inline — select ZAR, XLM, or USDC. For cross-border, the app converts via Stellar in real-time and the recipient gets local currency (GHS, RWF) to their MoMo wallet in under 60 seconds.

04

Track & grow

Every transaction auto-categorized by AI. Budget by category. Track crypto portfolio value in ZAR equivalent. Swap assets on the Stellar DEX. Export statements for tax or compliance.

About

Built by Africans,
for Africans.

Lumens Money is an African-first, privacy-native fintech platform that fuses Signal-grade end-to-end encrypted messaging with a non-custodial multi-asset crypto wallet and compliant fiat payment rails.

The core thesis: Money should move as freely as a message. Lumens eliminates the boundary between conversation and transaction — users send encrypted messages and GHS, ZAR, XLM, or USDC in the same thread, settling on Stellar in seconds at near-zero cost.

Terms & Conditions

Last updated: June 2026

1. Acceptance of Terms

By accessing or using Lumens Money, you agree to be bound by these Terms and Conditions and all applicable laws and regulations. If you do not agree, please do not use our services.

2. Description of Service

Lumens Money is an African-first financial messaging platform providing end-to-end encrypted messaging, a non-custodial multi-asset crypto wallet (Stellar XLM/USDC/XRP), ZAR digital wallet linked to a virtual card, cross-border payment rails (SA ↔ Ghana ↔ Rwanda), and AI-powered budget and expense tracking.

3. Eligibility & KYC

You must be 18 years or older and resident in a supported jurisdiction (South Africa, Ghana, Rwanda). KYC verification is required under FSCA, FICA (SA), BoG (Ghana), and BNR (Rwanda) requirements. Tier 1 KYC applies SA ID or Ghana Card; Tier 2 requires biometric liveness check via Smile Identity.

4. Non-Custodial Crypto Wallet

Your Stellar wallet private keys are generated and stored on your device using AES-256-GCM encryption. Lumens Money never holds or accesses your private keys. Crypto transactions on the Stellar network are irreversible. You are solely responsible for safeguarding your seed phrase and PIN.

5. ZAR Wallet & Virtual Card

Your ZAR digital wallet stores fiat currency and is linked to a virtual card. Lumens Money is not a bank and is not covered by the South African Deposit Insurance Corporation (SADIC) unless otherwise stated. Card usage is subject to terms of the issuing institution.

6. Cross-Border Payments

Lumens facilitates cross-border payments via Stellar settlement (USDC bridge) and local fiat rails (Paystack, Zeepay, GHIPSS MMI). Exchange rates and fees are shown at time of transaction. Processing times target under 60 seconds but may vary due to network conditions. Lumens operates in compliance with SARB cross-border mandates, BoG VASP requirements, and FIC AML regulations.

7. Prohibited Activities

  • Using Lumens for money laundering, terrorism financing, or sanctions evasion
  • Providing false or misleading KYC information
  • Circumventing security or rate-limiting measures
  • Using automated systems to abuse the platform
  • Transacting with sanctioned persons or entities (OFAC, UN, SARB lists)

8. POPIA & Data Residency (SA)

All South African user data is stored on servers located in Johannesburg, South Africa, in compliance with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) 2013. You have the right to access, correct, and request deletion of your personal information. Contact our Information Officer at popia@lumens.money.

9. Ghana Data Protection

Ghana user data is processed in compliance with the Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843). Lumens GH is registered with the Data Protection Commission of Ghana.

10. Limitation of Liability

Lumens Money shall not be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, including losses arising from market volatility of crypto assets, network congestion, third-party service failures, or force majeure events.

11. Governing Law

These terms are governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa. Disputes shall be resolved through arbitration in Johannesburg, South Africa, under the AFSA Arbitration Rules. Ghana users may escalate to the BoG Consumer Protection Unit.

12. Amendments

Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

1. Privacy-by-Design

Lumens Money is built privacy-native from the ground up. All messages are end-to-end encrypted using AES-256-GCM via the Web Crypto API — Lumens cannot read your messages. Private keys for your Stellar wallet are generated and stored locally on your device; Lumens never accesses or stores them on our servers.

2. Information We Collect

We collect: (a) Registration data — name, email, phone number, country of residence; (b) KYC verification data — government ID metadata, selfie biometric (processed by Smile Identity / Ghana NIA, raw biometric data is not stored by Lumens); (c) Transaction metadata — amounts, timestamps, counterparty wallet addresses (never message content); (d) Device and usage data — app version, session data, crash diagnostics; (e) Chat metadata — message delivery status and timestamps (never message content, which remains E2EE).

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To provide, maintain, and improve our services
  • To process transactions and manage wallets
  • To verify identity under FICA (SA), BoG AML directives (Ghana), and BNR requirements (Rwanda)
  • To detect and prevent fraud and AML/CFT violations
  • To communicate about your account, service updates, and regulatory changes
  • To generate anonymized, aggregated product analytics

4. Data Residency

South Africa: All SA user data is stored on self-hosted Supabase infrastructure in Johannesburg, complying with POPIA 2013 and SARB requirements. Ghana: Ghana user data stored in Accra / AWS af-west-1 (planned), complying with Data Protection Act 843. Rwanda: Data processed under BoG–BNR passport agreement and BNR data localisation rules.

5. Data Sharing

We share data only with: (a) KYC providers — Smile Identity (SA), Ghana NIA (GH), NIDA (RW) — only as required for identity verification; (b) Payment processors — Paystack, VALR B2B, Zeepay — only transaction data required for settlement; (c) Regulators — FIC (SA), FIC Ghana, BoG, BNR, SARB — only when required by law and only the minimum required. We never sell personal data to third parties or use it for advertising.

6. Blockchain Transparency

Stellar blockchain transactions are public by design. Your wallet address and transaction amounts are visible on the public Stellar ledger (e.g. Stellar Expert). Lumens cannot make blockchain transactions private. We do not link your legal identity to your wallet address in any public disclosure or third-party data sale.

7. Your Rights (POPIA / Act 843 / BNR)

  • Right to access your personal data held by Lumens
  • Right to correct inaccurate or outdated data
  • Right to deletion — subject to 5-year regulatory retention requirements under FICA and AML laws
  • Right to data portability — request a machine-readable export
  • Right to object to processing for direct marketing
  • Right to lodge a complaint with the SA Information Regulator or Data Protection Commission Ghana

8. Retention Periods

Transaction records: 5 years as required by FICA (SA) and BoG AML regulations. KYC documents: 5 years post account closure. Chat metadata (delivery/timestamps): 12 months, then deleted. Message content: Never stored (E2EE — device-only). You may request deletion of non-regulatory data at any time via the app or at privacy@lumens.money.

9. Security

We use AES-256-GCM message encryption, PBKDF2 PIN-based key derivation, TLS 1.3 in transit, row-level security (RLS) on all database tables, and regularly commission third-party security audits. We follow responsible disclosure for vulnerability reports at security@lumens.money.

10. Contact

Privacy inquiries: privacy@lumens.money. SA Information Officer (POPIA): popia@lumens.money. Ghana Data Protection contact: dpo.gh@lumens.money

Legal Notice

1. Company Information

Lumens Money is operated by Lumens Money (Pty) Ltd, incorporated in South Africa (Registration No. pending CIPC). Ghana operations are conducted through Lumens GH Ltd, incorporated at the Registrar General's Department, Accra, Ghana. Both entities share the same beneficial ownership structure.

2. Regulatory Status — South Africa

Lumens Technologies (Pty) Ltd is in the process of registering as a Payment Service Provider (PSP) with the Financial Sector Conduct Authority (FSCA) under the Financial Sector Regulation Act 9 of 2017. Lumens is registered with the Financial Intelligence Centre (FIC) as an accountable institution under the FIC Act 38 of 2001. Cross-border services operate under SARB cross-border payment frameworks. Full POPIA compliance is maintained.

3. Regulatory Status — Ghana

Lumens Labs GH Ltd is registered as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) with the Bank of Ghana (BoG) under the Payment Systems and Services Act 2019 (Act 987). PSP Medium licence application is in progress with the BoG. Data Protection Commission registration under Act 843 is completed. FIC Ghana AML/CFT registration is in progress.

4. Rwanda Licence Passport

Lumens Money benefits from the bilateral fintech licence passporting agreement between the Bank of Ghana (BoG) and the National Bank of Rwanda (BNR), signed 25 February 2025. Upon grant of the Ghana PSP licence, Lumens is entitled to extend operations to Rwanda under BNR notification procedures, without a full separate licence application.

5. Cryptocurrency Risk Disclaimer

Cryptocurrency assets including XLM, USDC, USDT, BTC, ETH, XRP, SOL, and ADA are highly volatile. Their value may decrease significantly or become zero. Lumens Money does not provide investment, financial, or legal advice. Nothing in this platform constitutes a solicitation to buy or sell digital assets. You should only hold crypto assets you can afford to lose and consult an independent financial adviser before making significant investment decisions.

6. Non-Bank Statement

Lumens Money is not a bank or deposit-taking institution. Lumens does not accept deposits within the meaning of the Banks Act 94 of 1990 (SA) or the Banks and Specialised Deposit-Taking Institutions Act 2023 (Ghana). ZAR digital wallet balances are not bank deposits and are not covered by SADIC deposit insurance unless explicitly stated in a separate agreement.

7. AML / CFT Compliance

Lumens Money maintains a Board-approved AML/CFT policy, reviewed annually. Transaction monitoring is performed in real-time using rule-based and ML-assisted screening. Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) are filed with the FIC (SA) and FIC Ghana as required. Lumens screens all users against OFAC SDN, UN Consolidated, SARB, BoG, and EU sanctions lists at onboarding and on an ongoing basis.

9. Dispute Resolution
